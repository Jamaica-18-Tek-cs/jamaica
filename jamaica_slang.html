  • Slang: Weh yuh ah seh Translation: How are you doing? Definition: When used as a greeting this expression means “How are you doing?”. The literal translation though is “What are you saying”. The shorter version of “weh yuh ah seh” is “weh yaw seh”. Example Sentences: Dialect: Weh yuh a seh? Mi deh try call yuh since mondeh Translation: How are you doing? I’ve been trying to call you since Monday?

  • Slang:Inna di morrows
    Translation: See you tomorrow Definition:Slang expression for saying goodbye to someone. The literal translation would be “In the tomorrows”.
    Example Sentences:Dialect: Mi a leff, Inna di morrows Translation: I’m leaving, See you tomorrow

  • Slang: Dawg nyam yu suppa Translation: Dog will eat your supper Definition: You will be punished/Something bad will happen to you. This saying usually relates to the consequences of an action or situation. Example Sentence: "Yu forget yu wife birthday? Dawg nyam yu suppa!" Translation: This indicates that there's an awful consequence to forgetting your wife's birthday.
